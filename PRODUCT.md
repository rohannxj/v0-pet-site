# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences sharing one storefront:

- **Trade buyers** — pet shop owners, retailers, vet practices buying wholesale. Context: desktop, procurement mindset, comparing prices and SKUs, wants account features and bulk pricing clarity.
- **Retail customers** — pet owners buying for their animals. Context: mobile-first, browsing by species/need, emotionally connected to their pets.

Both need confidence that Pet Vital is a credible, established supplier — not a dropshipper or marketplace.

## Product Purpose

Pet Vital is a UK-based wholesale and retail pet supplies distributor. It sells across dogs, cats, small animals, birds, fish, reptiles, and equine. Success means: trade buyers trust it enough to set up an account; retail buyers trust it enough to complete a purchase. The site must communicate institutional reliability while remaining approachable to pet owners.

## Brand Personality

Professional, reliable, trustworthy.

Voice: direct and knowledgeable, not excitable. Speaks like a supplier that has been in the industry for years — steady, helpful, no hype. Warm toward animals; businesslike toward the transaction.

## Anti-references

- Not a tech startup. No gradients-on-everything, no neon, no "disrupting pet care" energy.
- Not Amazon or eBay. No cluttered listing-grid walls, no aggressive orange CTAs, no race-to-the-bottom visual noise.
- Not Pets at Home. Avoid the big-box retail loudness — busy promotional banners, competing colours everywhere.
- Not a veterinary clinic. Clinical white + sterile typography undersells the warmth toward animals.

## Design Principles

1. **Institutional confidence first.** Every surface should feel like it was built by a company that has shipped a million orders. Reliability is earned through visual consistency and restraint, not decoration.
2. **Species as navigation, not taxonomy.** Users think "I need something for my dog" — lead with the animal, not the product category.
3. **Trade and retail coexist without friction.** Trade account features (bulk pricing, account management) should be prominent but not exclude casual buyers.
4. **Earned warmth.** The emotional connection to pets is real — let photography and copy carry it. The UI stays composed.
5. **Mobile as the retail baseline.** Retail buyers browse on phone. Trade buyers confirm on desktop. Optimise the primary flow for mobile without degrading the desktop trade experience.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum.
- Mobile-first responsive — full feature parity on small screens.
- Touch targets ≥44px on interactive elements.
